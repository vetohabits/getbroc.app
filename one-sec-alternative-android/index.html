one sec alternatives for Android

The breathing pause genuinely works, until the day you tap through it without noticing. Here's what comes after.

Credit where due

one sec deserves its reputation. The idea (one deliberate breath before a distracting app opens) has actual research behind it, and for autopilot opens it's remarkably effective at the start. Disclosure before we go further: Broc is our app, and this page exists for the people the pause stopped holding.

The habituation problem

Any interruption you see forty times a week gets absorbed into the ritual. Week one, the breath makes you reconsider. Week six, your thumb rides the animation like a yellow light you've decided to run. The psychology term is habituation; the practical term is "I tap through it now."

This isn't a defect in one sec or in you. Every friction-based tool decays the same way. The question is what you escalate to when it does.

The escalation ladder

Three genuinely different rungs above a pause:

Heavier friction: ScreenZen

Free, with pauses that can grow longer per open, plus usage goals and short-form-feed blocking. More friction, same theory. If you tapped through one sec, expect to tap through this eventually too, but the price is right. Comparison: ScreenZen vs Broc.

Hard schedules: Stay Focused

Timer and schedule logic with a strict mode that locks its own settings. Good if your weak hours are predictable. It still ends every block with nothing built.

A different deal entirely: Broc

Broc doesn't slow the open down; it changes what opening costs. Blocked apps show your habit list, and the feed unlocks when a habit is done: read, train, study, verified by an AI photo check or an in-app timer. There's no pause to habituate to because there's no pause. The unlock is the habit. That's the earned screen time model, and it doesn't decay the way friction does, because the reward keeps being real.
