Earn your screen time

Limits ration the scroll. Earning flips it: the scroll only exists because you did something first.

The idea in one paragraph

Every limit-based tool treats screen time as the default and your goals as the interruption. Earned screen time reverses that. Your distracting apps start the day locked. You do a real habit: read, train, study, clean. The habit unlocks minutes. Scroll them guilt-free, and when they're gone, the wall comes back until the next habit. The apps you crave stop competing with your day and start paying for it.

The craving doesn't go away, and it doesn't have to. It switches sides. Wanting to scroll becomes the reason the workout happens.

Why this beats rationing

A daily limit answers the wrong question. "How much scrolling is too much?" invites negotiation, and you always win negotiations against yourself. Earned time answers a better question: "what has to be true before the feed opens?" That's not a negotiation, it's a checklist. It also fixes the guilt problem: scrolling you earned feels like a break, not a relapse.

There's a name for the pattern this borrows: reward substitution. Put the payoff you want today (the scroll) behind the behaviour that pays off in a year (the habit), and the habit inherits the craving. Casinos use the trick against you. This points it the other way.

Apps that use the earn mechanic

The idea is catching on, with different definitions of "earning":

  • Broc (Android): earn time by completing verified daily habits. The habit itself is the currency.
  • Unrot (iPhone): the app that made the mechanic famous. Habits earn coins through timers or photo checks, and coins unlock your apps. iPhone only; there's no official Android version.
  • Forest and other gamified blockers: "earn" virtual trees or points for staying off the phone. The stake is imaginary, which is exactly why it stops working once the novelty fades.
  • ScreenZen / one sec: not earning at all. They add pauses and prompts before the app opens. Better than nothing; still a delay you can outlast.

Common questions

What counts as a habit?

Anything concrete and finishable: pages read, a workout, a meditation, a study block, dishes done, the dog walked. "Be more productive" is not a habit. "20 flashcards" is.

What stops me lying?

Verification. The photo check wants to see the open book or the gym before the habit counts. The timer pauses the moment you leave the app. And the streak makes honesty cheaper than the shortcut, day after day.
