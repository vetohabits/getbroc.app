How to block TikTok until you finish studying

You opened TikTok for 5 minutes. It's been 47. Here's a setup where the feed physically doesn't open until the study session is done.

Why "I'll just delete it before exams" never sticks

Deleting the app feels decisive. It's also completely free to undo: reinstalling takes under a minute, nobody sees it, and the algorithm remembers you fondly. So the decision gets remade every single time willpower dips, and exam season is one long willpower dip.

Screen-time limits have the same hole. When the timer runs out, a dialog asks you to stop, and you hold the "ignore limit" button. The person who set the rule can waive the rule for free. That's not a system, it's a suggestion.

The fix is changing what the exit costs. Instead of a block you can dismiss, use a block that ends when the studying is done.

The setup (about 3 minutes)

Broc is a habit-based app blocker: block distracting apps, do a real habit, prove it with a photo, earn the unlock. For studying it looks like this:

Add a study habit

Make it concrete and finishable: "45 minutes of past papers", "one lecture summarised", "20 flashcards". Not "study more".

Block the apps that eat your sessions

TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, games. You pick the list. Opening any of them now shows your habit list instead of the feed.

Pick your unlock mode

Per habit: each finished habit earns a chunk of screen time (a study block earns a scroll break). All habits first: nothing opens until the whole list is cleared. Exam-week mode, basically.

Optional: make it cheat-proof

With Broc Pro, AI photo verification wants a photo of the work (your notes, the flashcards, the desk) before the habit counts. No ticking the box from bed.

Why this works when timers don't

A timer measures time in the app. It has no idea whether you studied. Flipping it around, so the scroll is the reward for the work, does two things at once: the distraction stops competing with the studying, and the studying gets a payoff today instead of in June. The craving for a break doesn't go away. It starts working for you.

Streaks do the long-term part. Every day the habits get done, the streak grows, and day 23 of a streak is genuinely hard to throw away over one scroll.
