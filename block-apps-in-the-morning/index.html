Block apps in the morning

Alarm off, thumb on Instagram, and suddenly it's 7:41 and you're reading a stranger's argument. The first hour deserves better.

Why the morning scroll owns you

The deck is stacked three ways before you're even awake. You're at the lowest-willpower point of the day. The phone is already in your hand, because the alarm lives there. And the feeds have had all night to stack up novelty. "I'll just check notifications" at 6:50 becomes forty minutes of other people's mornings, and the day starts reactive, rushed and slightly behind, which is the mood the rest of it inherits.

Deciding not to scroll each morning is re-fighting the same battle daily at your weakest hour. Deciding once that mornings are locked is a different category of decision, made when you're strong.

The setup: mornings behind the wall

Block the morning offenders

Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, news apps, and be honest about email if email is your feed. They'll show Broc's wall instead of content.

Build a short, real morning list

Three finishable habits: make the bed (photo), 10 minutes of stretching or a walk (timer), 10 pages with coffee (photo). Small enough to always be doable, real enough to change the morning.

Use all-habits-first mode

Nothing unlocks until the whole list is done. The reach-and-scroll reflex hits the wall at 6:50; by 7:30 the bed is made, you've moved, and the feed opens for a coffee break you earned.

Move the alarm if you can

A cheap clock or a smartwatch alarm keeps the phone out of arm's reach entirely. The wall handles the phone; distance handles the half-asleep grab.

What changes, concretely

The habits themselves take 25 minutes. What you're actually buying is the tone: a morning that starts with three completed things instead of an hour of input you didn't choose. The wall resets daily, automatically: every sunrise it's back, yesterday's minutes are spent, and the streak ticks up with each morning you clear the list. After a couple of weeks the 6:50 grab starts skipping the locked apps on its own; reflexes only survive if they pay.

Variations

  • The gentler version: per-habit mode instead. Each morning habit unlocks a few minutes, so coffee-scrolling exists but has to be bought first.
  • The commuter version: make the third habit "leave the house"; verify with a photo from outside. The feed becomes a train-platform reward.
  • The heavy version: pair with the workout gate: mornings unlock only after training. Brutal, effective, not for week one.

Common questions

What if I need my phone for work at 7am?

Block the feeds, not the phone. Email, Slack, maps and banking stay available; Broc only walls the apps you choose.

Doesn't checking the weather lead to checking everything?

Not anymore: the everything-apps are the ones behind the wall. That's the difference between blocking apps and gritting teeth.
