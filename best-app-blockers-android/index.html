The short version of each

Broc: the habit gate

Block the apps that pull you in; they stay locked until your daily habits are done. Habits are verified with an AI photo check or an in-app timer, each one earns screen time, and streaks make quitting expensive. It is the highest-friction habit gate in this comparison: there is no in-app snooze button, only a habit to do. Android only.

Stay Focused: the scheduler

The power tool: per-app limits, hourly schedules, launch counts, and a strict mode that locks its own settings. If your life runs on a timetable, it's a strong pick. It's still timer logic: when the block ends, nothing was built.

AppBlock: the all-rounder

Clean interface, app and website blocking, location and Wi-Fi based rules. Comparable territory to Stay Focused; pick whichever interface you prefer.

minimalist phone: the launcher

Replaces your home screen with a text-only list and adds friction at the launcher level. Changes how the whole phone feels rather than blocking specific apps. Pairs surprisingly well with a blocker.

ScreenZen: the free pause

Pauses, reflection prompts, usage goals, and notably: blocking just the short-form feeds (Reels, Shorts) inside apps. The best free starting point. Our full head-to-head: ScreenZen vs Broc.

one sec: the breath

One deliberate breathing exercise before the app opens, with research behind the approach. Great for surfacing the autopilot open; gentle by design, and gentle is exactly what some people need. Not what most of our readers need by the time they're searching for lists like this.

Forest: the tree

Plant a virtual tree, stay off the phone or it dies. Lovely for timed focus sessions, especially with friends. As an always-on blocker the stake is imaginary, and week four you know it.

The honest decision tree

  • Never tried anything? Start free: ScreenZen.
  • Need schedules and a settings lock? Stay Focused or AppBlock.
  • Want the whole phone calmer, not just blocked? minimalist phone.
  • Timed deep-work sessions with a friend? Forest.
  • Bypassed all of the above? That's the gap Broc exists for: a habit app blocker where the only way through is doing something real.

Common questions

Is there one that can't be uninstalled?

Broc does not prevent uninstall. Some Android device-management modes and strict blockers can restrict uninstalling or settings changes, but the details vary. For a normal consumer setup, check exactly which protections are offered and what recovery path remains.

What does Broc cost?

Broc is a subscription with a 7-day free trial. One plan, everything in: unlimited habits, custom unlock durations, AI photo verification and full history. No ads.
