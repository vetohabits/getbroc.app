How to block Instagram Reels on Android

You opened Instagram to check one message. Reels had other plans. Two honest ways to end that, depending on how deep the problem goes.

First: Instagram won't help you

There is no setting inside Instagram to turn Reels off. Not hidden, not buried: it doesn't exist, because Reels is the most engaging surface in the app and engagement is the business. "Not interested" on individual clips tweaks the algorithm; the feed itself is permanent. Quiet mode and daily limits are the same dismissible timers you've already learned to ignore.

So the real options live outside Instagram, and there are exactly two, depending on your diagnosis.

Option 1: cut Reels specifically, keep the rest

Best if: DMs and posts are genuinely useful to you, and Reels is the only trap.

Some blockers can detect and block the short-form surfaces inside apps. ScreenZen (free) is the best known: it can block the Reels feed (and YouTube Shorts, and TikTok) while leaving the rest of Instagram alone. You tap into Reels and ScreenZen steps in front of it.

The honest caveat: this is friction, not a wall. The block screen has options you can tap through, and week-six-you taps faster than week-one-you. If your Reels problem is a reflex, this may be all you need. If it's a craving, read on.

Option 2: make Instagram itself earned

Best if: "I'll just check my DMs" is how every Reels binge starts, and you know it.

Broc doesn't do surgical Reels removal. It does something blunter and, for drifters, more effective: Instagram stays locked until your daily habits are done. Open it early and you get the wall: your habit list and what each one earns. Do the reading or the workout (verified by an AI photo check or a timer), and Instagram opens for the minutes you earned, Reels included.
